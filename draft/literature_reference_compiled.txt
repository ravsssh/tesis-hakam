Sitasi: Kung, J. J., Carverhill, A. P., & Mcleod, R. H. (2010). Indonesia’s stock market: Evolving role, growing efficiency. Bulletin of Indonesian Economic Studies, 46(3), 329–346. https://doi.org/10.1080/00074918.2010.522503
The document discusses the evolution and increasing efficiency of Indonesia's stock market, particularly following a series of reforms and deregulation measures implemented from 1987 onwards.
Overview of Indonesia's Stock Market Evolution
The article discusses the transformation of Indonesia's stock market from a period of inactivity to a significant player in the country's financial system, particularly following reforms initiated in the late 1980s. It highlights the growing efficiency and importance of the stock market relative to the banking sector.
Historical Context of Indonesia's Stock Market
The Jakarta Stock Exchange (JSX) was re-opened in 1977 after two decades of inactivity.
Initial growth was limited, with only 24 stocks listed from 1977 to 1984.
The market was primarily government-driven, with little public participation and low per capita incomes.
Impact of Economic Reforms on Stock Market
Economic reforms began in December 1987, known as Pakdes I, aimed at revitalizing the stock market.
Deregulation measures included lifting prohibitions on foreign ownership of shares and enhancing private sector involvement.
The stock market's activity surged post-reforms, with the Jakarta Composite Index rising from 80 in 1987 to 637 by 1996.
Changing Dynamics Between Banks and Stock Market
The stock market's role grew significantly, surpassing bank loans in total value by 2004.
The 1997-98 financial crisis highlighted the risks of over-reliance on banks, prompting a shift towards diversifying financial intermediation.
The government’s deregulation efforts allowed the stock market to play a more substantial role in funding and investment.
Efficiency of the Stock Market
The study examines the effectiveness of technical trading rules, such as moving averages and trading range breakouts.
Results indicate that investors could achieve above-average returns using these strategies, particularly in the earlier sub-period (1988-1996).
The efficiency of the market improved over time, with a notable decline in the predictability of returns in the later period (1999-2007).
Methodology for Analyzing Stock Market Performance
The analysis utilized daily return data from the Jakarta Composite Index, excluding the crisis years of 1997-98.
Two technical trading strategies were employed to assess their effectiveness in generating returns.
One-tailed hypothesis tests were conducted to compare mean returns from trading signals against unconditional mean returns.
Summary of Trading Strategies and Results
Moving average rules in the 1988-96 period yielded high mean daily returns, averaging 0.00199, significantly above the unconditional mean of 0.00087.
In contrast, the 1999-2007 period showed diminished returns, with mean daily returns for buys averaging 0.00121, closely aligning with the unconditional mean.
Trading range breakout rules also demonstrated strong performance in the earlier period but underperformed in the later period, with average returns for buys at 0.00518.
Conclusion on Stock Market's Role in Economic Development
The stock market's contribution to Indonesia's economic development has increased dramatically since the late 1980s.
Policy changes have facilitated private sector participation and foreign investment, enhancing market efficiency.
The reforms have laid a strong foundation for the stock market, although returns have stabilized closer to average levels in recent years.
ASEAN Finance Ministers Meetings
Joint ministerial statements from various ASEAN countries highlight financial cooperation.
Meetings took place in locations such as Prague, Vientiane, Yangon, and Hanoi between 1999 and 2005.
Key Financial Literature
Notable works include Greenspan's lessons from global crises and Levine & Zervos on stock markets and economic growth.
Other significant studies focus on banking deregulation in Indonesia and the role of non-bank financial institutions.
Major Reform and Deregulation Measures
The 1987 Pakdes package allowed stock prices to move according to market forces.
Key reforms included the establishment of the Indonesian Central Securities Depository in 1997 and the merger of stock exchanges in 2007.

- CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) adalah metodologi yang paling banyak digunakan untuk proyek data mining dan machine learning, meskipun telah berusia lebih dari 20 tahun
- CRISP-DM terdiri dari 6 fase: Business Understanding, Data Understanding, Data Preparation, Modeling, Evaluation, dan Deployment
- Metodologi ini bersifat domain-independent dan tidak terikat pada teknologi tertentu, sehingga dapat diterapkan pada berbagai jenis proyek analitik data
- Systematic literature review mengidentifikasi bahwa fase Deployment sering kali tidak dilakukan atau tidak dilaporkan dalam penelitian akademis—hanya sedikit studi yang mengimplementasikan model ke lingkungan produksi

Sitasi: Principles of Economics Ninth Edition N. Gregory Mankiw

Chapter 1-2
Rangkuman Chapter 1-2: Inflasi, Suku Bunga, Nilai Tukar, Money Supply, dan Sistem Perbankan

1. Inflasi
(1) Definisi Teoretis:
Inflasi adalah peningkatan umum dalam tingkat harga barang dan jasa di suatu ekonomi. ​ Inflasi biasanya terjadi ketika jumlah uang yang beredar meningkat secara signifikan, sehingga nilai uang menurun. ​
(2) Mekanisme Dampak ke Asset Prices/Pasar Keuangan:

Inflasi yang tinggi dapat mengurangi daya beli konsumen, sehingga menekan pendapatan perusahaan dan harga saham.
Inflasi yang tinggi sering kali mendorong bank sentral untuk menaikkan suku bunga guna mengendalikan kenaikan harga. Suku bunga yang lebih tinggi dapat menekan harga aset karena meningkatkan biaya pinjaman dan mengurangi daya tarik investasi di pasar saham.
Inflasi yang terkendali dapat mendukung pertumbuhan ekonomi, yang pada gilirannya dapat meningkatkan kepercayaan investor dan harga saham.

(3) Kutipan Penting:
"Inflation is caused primarily by too much growth in the money supply." ​ (Chapter 2, Key Concepts)

2. Suku Bunga
(1) Definisi Teoretis:
Suku bunga adalah biaya yang dikenakan atas penggunaan uang yang dipinjam, biasanya dinyatakan sebagai persentase dari jumlah pinjaman. Suku bunga ditentukan oleh bank sentral dan memengaruhi tingkat pengeluaran, investasi, dan tabungan dalam ekonomi.
(2) Mekanisme Dampak ke Asset Prices/Pasar Keuangan:

Kenaikan suku bunga biasanya menyebabkan penurunan harga saham karena meningkatkan biaya pinjaman bagi perusahaan dan mengurangi daya tarik investasi di pasar saham.
Suku bunga yang lebih tinggi juga dapat mendorong investor untuk beralih ke instrumen investasi berbasis bunga, seperti obligasi, yang lebih menarik dibandingkan saham.
Suku bunga yang rendah dapat meningkatkan likuiditas di pasar keuangan, mendorong investasi, dan meningkatkan harga saham.

(3) Kutipan Penting:
"Policymakers can exploit the short-run trade-off between inflation and unemployment using various policy instruments, including changing the amount of money printed and adjusting interest rates." ​ (Chapter 1, Principle 10)

3. Nilai Tukar
(1) Definisi Teoretis:
Nilai tukar adalah harga relatif antara mata uang dua negara. Nilai tukar dapat bersifat tetap (fixed exchange rate) atau mengambang (floating exchange rate), tergantung pada kebijakan moneter negara tersebut.
(2) Mekanisme Dampak ke Asset Prices/Pasar Keuangan:

Perubahan nilai tukar memengaruhi daya saing ekspor dan impor suatu negara, yang dapat berdampak pada pendapatan perusahaan yang terdaftar di bursa saham.
Depresiasi nilai tukar dapat meningkatkan daya saing ekspor, tetapi juga meningkatkan biaya impor, yang dapat memengaruhi inflasi dan profitabilitas perusahaan.
Apresiasi nilai tukar dapat menekan daya saing ekspor, tetapi mengurangi biaya impor, yang dapat menguntungkan perusahaan yang bergantung pada bahan baku impor.

(3) Kutipan Penting:
"Flexible and floating exchange rates offer an effective international monetary arrangement." ​ (Chapter 2, Propositions about Which Most Economists Agree) ​

4. Money Supply (Jumlah Uang Beredar)
(1) Definisi Teoretis:
Money supply adalah total jumlah uang yang beredar dalam suatu ekonomi, termasuk uang tunai dan simpanan di bank. Pertumbuhan jumlah uang beredar yang berlebihan dapat menyebabkan inflasi. ​
(2) Mekanisme Dampak ke Asset Prices/Pasar Keuangan:

Definition of Stock Market
The stock market is a financial market where shares of publicly traded companies are bought and sold. ​ It serves as a platform for companies to raise capital and for investors to buy ownership stakes in businesses.

Key Theories Related to Stock Market ​
1. Efficient Markets Hypothesis (EMH) ​

Definition: The EMH posits that stock prices reflect all publicly available information about the value of an asset. ​ As a result, stocks are always fairly valued, and it is impossible to consistently outperform the market through stock selection or market timing. ​
Implications:

Stock prices follow a random walk, meaning changes in stock prices are unpredictable and driven by new information. ​
Actively managed funds often fail to outperform index funds, which passively track the market. ​


Key Quote: "According to the efficient markets hypothesis, financial markets process available information rationally, so a stock price always equals the best estimate of the value of the underlying business." ​


2. Fundamental Analysis

Definition: Fundamental analysis involves studying a company’s financial statements, market conditions, and future prospects to determine its intrinsic value. ​
Implications:

Investors use fundamental analysis to identify undervalued stocks (price < value) and overvalued stocks (price > value). ​
The value of a stock is determined by the present value of expected future cash flows, including dividends and the final sale price. ​


Key Quote: "The value of a stock to a stockholder is what she gets out of owning it, which includes the present value of the stream of dividend payments and the final sale price." ​


3. Risk and Return Trade-Off ​

Definition: Investors face a trade-off between risk and return. ​ Riskier assets, such as stocks, tend to offer higher average returns to compensate for the uncertainty. ​
Implications:

Stocks are riskier than bonds but historically offer higher average returns. ​
Diversification reduces firm-specific risk (risk tied to individual companies) but cannot eliminate market risk (risk tied to overall economic conditions). ​


Key Quote: "When people increase the percentage of their savings that they have invested in stocks, they increase the average return they can expect to earn, but they also increase the risks they face." ​


4. Stock Valuation

Definition: The value of a stock is the present value of expected future cash flows, including dividends and the final sale price. ​
Implications:

Stock prices are influenced by factors such as company profitability, competition, customer loyalty, government regulations, and economic conditions. ​
Speculative bubbles can occur when stock prices rise above their fundamental value due to irrational investor behavior. ​


Key Quote: "Whenever the price of an asset rises above what appears to be its fundamental value, the market is said to be experiencing a speculative bubble." ​


Conclusion
The stock market is a critical component of the financial system, linking savers and investors. ​ Theories such as the Efficient Markets Hypothesis, Fundamental Analysis, and the Risk-Return Trade-Off provide foundational insights into how stock prices are determined and how investors can approach stock market decisions. While the efficient markets hypothesis suggests that beating the market is nearly impossible, diversification and understanding risk can help investors make informed decisions. ​

Korelasi antara pengembalian harga komoditas dan saham bersifat dinamis dan sangat volatil, terutama setelah krisis keuangan 2007-2008. ​
Krisis keuangan 2007-2008 memperkuat hubungan antara pasar komoditas dan saham, mencerminkan financialization of commodity markets. ​
Komoditas tidak dapat dianggap sebagai kelas aset yang homogen karena dipengaruhi oleh faktor makroekonomi umum dan determinan pasar spesifik.
Fenomena spekulasi terlihat pada minyak, kopi, dan kakao, dengan korelasi yang meningkat selama harga saham naik dan menurun selama pasar saham bearish. ​
Emas menunjukkan peran sebagai safe-haven dengan korelasi negatif terhadap pengembalian saham, terutama selama periode penurunan harga saham. ​


(7) Kutipan Penting

Fokus pada Mekanisme Transmisi Komoditas ke Equity Markets ​
Krisis keuangan 2007-2008 mengubah hubungan antara pasar komoditas dan saham, dengan korelasi yang meningkat selama periode bullish dan menurun selama periode bearish. ​ Fenomena ini menunjukkan bahwa komoditas dapat berfungsi sebagai substitusi atau pelengkap dalam portofolio investasi, tergantung pada kondisi pasar. ​ Financialization of commodity markets juga memperkuat hubungan ini, dengan spekulasi yang memengaruhi volatilitas harga komoditas dan saham. ​
